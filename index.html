What Makes a Roofing Contractor “Reliable”?

We’ve learned that reliability comes down to a few core principles—ones that we apply to every job we take on:

1. Experience and Local Knowledge

A roofing contractor with years of experience and a solid understanding of local building codes, weather patterns, and homeowner concerns is more likely to deliver high-quality work. We’ve served communities across Texas, and we know what works—and what doesn’t—when it comes to weatherproofing and durability.

2. Clear Communication

From the first inspection to the final cleanup, you should always feel informed and supported. We make it a point to explain our process, provide honest timelines, and update you regularly so you’re never left wondering what’s next.

3. Proper Licensing and Insurance

Always verify that your roofing contractor is fully licensed and insured. This protects both you and your property in case of accidents or unexpected issues. At Tidal Remodeling, we carry all required credentials and are happy to provide documentation on request.

4. Quality Materials

The best workmanship still won’t hold up if the materials aren’t up to par. That’s why we only use shingles, underlayment, and flashing from trusted manufacturers that meet our high standards for performance and longevity.

5. Transparent Pricing

No one likes hidden fees or last-minute surprises. We provide detailed estimates and walk you through them line by line. We’d rather over communicate than leave room for misunderstanding.

Signs It’s Time to Call a Roofing Contractor

You don’t always need to see a leak for roof damage to exist. Here are a few common signs that it might be time to give us a call:

  • Missing or curling shingles
  • Sagging areas on your roof
  • Granules collecting in gutters
  • Daylight visible through attic boards
  • Stains on ceilings or walls inside your home

We recommend scheduling a roof inspection every few years—even if everything seems fine. Preventative maintenance often costs far less than emergency repairs.

How to Vet a Roofing Contractor Before Signing

We always encourage homeowners to do their research. Whether you’re considering us or someone else, here are a few important steps to take:

  • Read Reviews and Testimonials
     Look for patterns in customer feedback. Are clients consistently happy with the results and communication?
  • Ask About Previous Projects
     A good contractor will gladly share before-and-after photos or connect you with past clients.
  • Request a Written Estimate
     Get everything in writing—including the scope of work, materials, warranty info, and cleanup details.
  • Check Licenses and Insurance
     This can’t be overstated. Protect yourself by verifying everything before work begins.

Roofing Materials We Trust and Use

We’ve tested a variety of materials over the years, and we only use the ones that consistently perform well under Texas heat, storms, and temperature fluctuations. Here’s a quick overview of some of our go-to options:

  • Asphalt Shingles – Budget-friendly and reliable for most homes.
  • Metal Roofing – Extremely durable, great for longevity and energy efficiency.
  • Tile Roofing – Stylish and strong, though heavier and best for certain roof structures.
  • Flat Roofing Systems – Ideal for commercial or modern residential buildings.

Common Roofing Questions We Get

We know roofing can be a bit of a mystery to most homeowners. Here are a few questions we hear regularly:

How long will my new roof last?

That depends on the material. Asphalt shingles typically last 15–30 years, while metal and tile roofs can last 50 years or more with proper care.

Can you work with my insurance company?

Yes, we frequently assist homeowners with insurance claims, especially for storm-related damage. We can help document everything and communicate with your adjuster.

How long does a typical roof replacement take?

Most residential jobs take 1–3 days, depending on size and complexity. We’ll give you an accurate timeline before we begin.

Do you offer warranties?

Absolutely. We stand by our work and provide both workmanship and manufacturer warranties for your peace of mind.
